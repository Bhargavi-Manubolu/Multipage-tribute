Tribute to Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was the first woman of Indian origin in space. She first flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a mission specialist and primary robotic arm operator. She lost her life in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster in 2003.

Chawla was born in Karnal, India, on July 1, 1961. She earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College before immigrating to the United States and becoming a naturalized citizen.

Throughout her life, Chawla served as an inspiration to millions, breaking barriers and pursuing her dreams in the face of adversity.
